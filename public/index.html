Sumo wrestling for a good cause

Take the chance to wrestle your favourite rikishi - and all for a good cause. The price includes not only the match, but also a practice session in the wrestler's beya, as well as your very own cotton mawashi.

All proceedings will go to Médecins Sans Frontiers in their effort to fight COVID-19.

はっけよい!

はっけよい!

Pronunciation: Hak-ke-yo-i

Meaning: "Put some spirit into it". The phrase is shouted by the sumo referee (also known as the gyoji) when the match has begun, or to encite the wrestlers into action whenever they reach a standstill.
